    What We Do

    We are creating a global community of teachers, students, and professionals who want to improve educational content. The Oasys platform provides authoring tools that allow everyone to contribute, even those without computer experience. We scale Oasys by combining our authoring tools with Artificial Intelligence and the Blockchain.

    Authoring Tools

    Decrease barriers to create or improve educational content.

    Artificial Intelligence

    Estimate the value each piece of educational content brings to the platform.

    Blockchain

    Record and distribute rewards for valuable contributions.

    Authoring Tools

    Anyone can create content for the Oasys Platform.

    Our tools modularize the development process. You can create reusable interactive simulations, pedagogical educational content, or both!

    Artificial Intelligence

    "Proof of Learning" algorithms automatically estimate how much value each piece of content is bringing to the community.

    This allows us to incentivize high quality content that effectively teaches students. Non-effective content will bring very little value to the platform.

    Blockchain

    All contributions are recorded on the blockchain.

    The blockchain allows us to easily track the contributions each individual makes. Once the AI has estimated the value of the contribution, the blockchain will automate payments to creators.




    Why We Do It

    We are empowering a community of educators to create the best educational content. We believe that offering higher quality, more accessible educational content can improve STEM proficiency, lower achievement gaps, and make learning more enjoyable.

    Improve STEM proficiency

    80% of high school students graduated without STEM proficiency in the US this year.

    Decrease Achievement Gaps

    White-black and white-hispanic achievement gaps in math and reading range from .5 to .9 standard deviations.

    Advance Learning Effectiveness

    Textbooks and lectures communicate less information than interactive learning experiences.
